# Topological Persistence

---

## Architectural Position

Topological persistence defines:
how recursive structures
remain continuous across time.

---

## Persistence Principle

Topology persists through:

- bounded memory
- recursive continuity
- sparse coupling
- controlled perturbation

---

## Persistence Constraints

Persistence may NOT become:

- frozen rigidity
- infinite accumulation
- total synchronisation

---

## Temporal Requirement

Persistence requires:

F(t+1) - F(t)

to remain bounded.

---

## Structural Purpose

Persistence preserves:
recursive continuity
without eliminating transformation.

---

## Final Statement

Topological persistence stabilises:
recursive structural identity
across temporal evolution.
