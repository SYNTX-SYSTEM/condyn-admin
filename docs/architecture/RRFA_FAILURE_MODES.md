# RRFA
# Failure Modes

---

## Architectural Position

Failure modes define:
how recursive architectures
lose structural integrity.

Most systems fail:
silently.

RRFA therefore models:
failure explicitly.

---

# Core Principle

Failure rarely appears:
suddenly.

Most recursive collapse
emerges gradually
through accumulated instability.

---

# Primary Failure Classes

RRFA identifies:
- semantic contamination
- recursive overload
- topology fragmentation
- propagation saturation
- renderer corruption
- ontology inflation
- masking blindness
- temporal collapse
- recursive rigidity

---

# Semantic Contamination

Semantics enter:
primitive space
or topology space.

Result:
RRFA collapses into analytics.

---

# Recursive Overload

Recursive activation exceeds:
stabilisation capacity.

Result:
field turbulence explosion.

---

# Fragmentation Collapse

Topology loses:
recursive continuity.

Result:
field separation.

---

# Propagation Saturation

Everything propagates everywhere.

Result:
loss of differentiation.

---

# Renderer Corruption

Renderer gains:
interpretive authority.

Result:
perceptual manipulation architecture.

---

# Ontology Inflation

New primitives added:
without orthogonality control.

Result:
recursive inconsistency.

---

# Masking Blindness

Instability remains:
hidden too long.

Result:
late stage collapse detection.

---

# Temporal Collapse

Recursive delay disappears.

Result:
simultaneous self reaction instability.

---

# Recursive Rigidity

System becomes:
too stable.

Result:
loss of emergence.

---

# Why Failure Matters

RRFA does not assume:
perfect operation.

Failure is structurally inevitable.

The goal is:
detectable bounded instability.

---

# Failure Constraints

Failure analysis may NEVER:
collapse into:
- morality
- blame
- semantic judgement
- psychological interpretation

Failure remains:
structural.

---

# Final Statement

Failure modes are the visible boundaries
of recursive structural stability.
