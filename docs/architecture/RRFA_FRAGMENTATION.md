# Fragmentation

## Architectural Position

Fragmentation defines:
the recursive loss
of relational continuity
inside topology.

Fragmentation is NOT:
conflict.

It is:
decreasing recursive coupling.

This distinction is fundamental.

---

# Core Principle

Topology fragments when:
recursive relational persistence
can no longer maintain
field continuity.

Fragmentation therefore emerges:
gradually.

Not instantly.

---

# Local vs Global Fragmentation

Local interruption
does NOT automatically imply:
global fragmentation.

RRFA separates:

- transient interruption
- recursive fragmentation

This prevents:
false instability detection.

---

# Fragmentation Formation

Fragmentation increases when:
- coupling weakens
- propagation breaks
- recursive continuity decays
- resonance corridors collapse
- masking delays adaptation

---

# Fragmentation And Drift

Persistent drift may:
separate relational topology
into increasingly disconnected regions.

Fragmentation therefore often emerges:
through accumulated drift.

---

# Fragmentation And Resonance

Loss of resonance continuity
accelerates fragmentation.

Strong resonance corridors:
temporarily resist fragmentation.

---

# Fragmentation And Memory

Field memory may preserve:
residual coupling
after active topology weakens.

This creates:
latent coherence zones.

---

# Fragmentation Thresholds

Fragmentation becomes structural when:
recursive reconnection probability
falls below continuity thresholds.

At this point:
the topology reorganises itself.

- imply hostility
- imply emotional failure
- imply moral breakdown
- define semantic meaning

Fragmentation remains:
purely relational.
