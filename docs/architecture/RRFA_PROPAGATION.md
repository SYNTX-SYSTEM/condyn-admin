# RRFA
# Propagation

---

## Architectural Position

Propagation defines:
how relational activation
moves through topology.

No recursive field remains local forever.

Topology transmits influence.

---

# Core Principle

Propagation is NOT:
message transfer.

Propagation is:
recursive transmission of relational disturbance.

This distinction is critical.

---

# Propagation Structure

Propagation depends on:
- topology density
- recursive coupling
- interruption
- masking
- curvature
- temporal delay
- resonance

The field therefore propagates:
non uniformly.

---

# Directionality

Propagation possesses:
directional asymmetry.

Recursive influence does not spread:
equally in all directions.

Topology bends propagation.

---

# Propagation Velocity

Different topologies
propagate recursively
at different temporal rates.

Examples:
- dense recursion accelerates propagation
- fragmentation slows propagation
- masking distorts propagation
- delay dampens propagation

---

# Propagation Thresholds

Not all relational disturbance propagates.

Weak local fluctuations may:
dissolve before recursive amplification occurs.

Propagation therefore requires:
minimum recursive persistence.

---

# Propagation And Resonance

Resonance amplifies:
propagation continuity.

Strong resonance corridors
allow:
long distance recursive activation.

---

# Propagation And Drift

Persistent asymmetric propagation
creates:
topological drift.

Drift therefore partially emerges through:
recursive propagation imbalance.

---

# Propagation Constraints

Propagation may NEVER:
- inject semantics
- infer meaning
- classify intention
- assign emotion

Propagation preserves:
recursive movement only.

---

# Failure Modes

## Uniform Propagation

All influence spreads equally.

Result:
loss of topology.

---

## Infinite Propagation

Nothing dissipates.

Result:
field saturation.

---

## Semantic Propagation

Meaning spreads instead of:
relational topology.

Result:
analytics regression.

---

# Final Statement

Propagation is the recursive movement
of relational disturbance
through dynamic topology.
